/ Puritan Code

The Puritan Codes

Failure to attend church

Religion was the most important aspect of Puritan culture. Because of this, they had strict rules concerning it, one of those was making church mandatory. Punishment would start at loss of food and end at whipping or even worse.

Blasphemy

Puritan society was essentially founded on their religion. As a result, anything disparaging towards their religion was taken very seriously. Punishment could be being forced to wear a "B", whipping, or even capital punishment.

Failure to pay taxes

Rules were a big part of Puritan society. So much so that just sacrificing what you owned was valued. As a result not paying taxes was treated extremely harshly just like in most modern societies. Taxes were extremely important to them however because the funds were used to support the church.

Witchcraft

A common trait of Puritans was superstition. Because of that and their combined hatred of anything that could be interpreted as evil, they hater witches. People believed to be witches were usually executed.

Drunkenness

Puritans valued appearance and collectedness. Public drunkeness was looked down on because it showed a lack of formality. The punishment was generally public humiliation in the form of wearing a letter "D". Some punishments that occured lasted from a day up to a year.

Adultery

Adultery is a crime in which a man or a woman has sex outside of marriage. It is punished by forcing the adulterer to wear a scarlet letter "A". And in more extreme instances, whipping or execution.
